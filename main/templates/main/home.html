Before You Buy

Don’t believe the lies like canceled buildings discount

We recommend some caution in considering purchasing any building whether it be farm, commercial, industrial, storage etc. All steel buildings are an excellent value with no maintenance and almost lifetime trouble free service. When choosing a steel building manufacturer, or dealing with a reputable dealer (we stress reputable) there are a number of points that you should be aware of. All buildings are not equal; in fact, some buildings do not even meet building code standards and could be a very poor investment. With steel buildings, like many things, you get what you pay for.

Don’t fall for sales pitches and pressure sales

Take time out to review the following checklist before you make your investment.

  • The drawings are stamped by an engineer, registered in good standing with the province in which you want to build. If you have any doubts about the engineer, call the association of provincial engineers (416-961-1100).
  • Foundation and reaction loads are supplied. They are required to properly design the foundation.
  • Roof and sidewalls are a minimum of 26 gauge steel with an AZI165 galvalume coating.
  • The roof and sidewalls are a minimum of 1-1/2 inch deep individual rib panel.
  • The manufacturer of the building is certified by the Canadian Welding Bureau. (416-542-1312)
  • All welding is done to Canadian Standard Association CSA W59.
  • Canadian Sheet Steel Building Institute certificate of design is supplied certifying the building is designed to the required building code and snow load.
  • Mill test reports are available for all the steel used in your building.
  • A most recent law also requires the building is CSA-A660 certified, or it would be illegal and the building department would reject it.
